CERN blackboard seminar rules:

  1. No slides.

* I mentioned that I come from a physics background, and I want to add an anecdote about blackboard seminar culture at CERN, which is the European Center for Nuclear Research and home of the LHC. You may remember that several years ago, LHC discovered the Higgs Boson. It was a big accomplishment; Nobel prizes were awarded. But in the physics community, CERN is also famous for its vibrant blackboard seminars, which germinate new ideas and bring together researchers from disparate fields. There are two strict rules for the blackboard seminars * 1) no slides. must use blackboard * 2) no discussion of your own published work, except to provide context for an open problem. In other words, show "raw" work and discuss what confuses you. * I think with the restrictions we're all under for COVID, we can abandon rule 1. You can see I've already broken it. * But I'm going to adhere to rule 2 and I wanted to put this disclaimer up front to say that, especially as a physicist talking to neuroscientitsts, I'm probably going to say some dumb stuff at some point here and really want you to tell me when I do that because it's the only way that I'm going to learn to differentiate between the known laws of physics and the laws of physics known to me. * My hope is that the talk is informative, but that at some point it morphs into more of a discussion than a lecture.
