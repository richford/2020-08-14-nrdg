Salience Network

McLaughlin et al. (2019)
- So most studies of emotional processing following adversity have concerned themselves with the amygdala. - But there's a network of regions the extend beyond the amygdala-mPFC circuit and respond to stimuli that have motivational salience but are not necessarily threatening. - This is called the salience network, good name, and includes the insula and dACC, among others. - These pie charts show differences in activation of salience network regions during threat processing tasks. We see null associations for deprivation and mixed adversity samples. - And some studies suggest positive associations with insula activation and negative associations with dACC activation. - Personally, I find it unsatisfying to motivate the study of the salience network by noting its importance to responsing to broader motivationally salient stimuli beyond threat processing, and then to study using only threat assessment tasks.
