The resilient tardigrade

  • Lack of adversity-related pathology, or even positive adaptation to it, is studied under the concept of resilience.
  • No agreed upon operational definition.
  • Awaits a conceptual framework that makes neurodevelopmental predictions.
- So those are the really open problems. Those are the kind that could easily occupy me for my postdoc and probably my whole career. - And then there's one massively confusing, big honkin' open problem that centers around this concept of resilience. - When I think of resilience, I think of the tardigrade here on the left, which can withstand a temperature range of 400 degrees, pressures from the vacuum of space to the bottom of the Marianas trench, and raditation levels of about 5000 Grays, which is about 1000 times more than what will kill a human. - And when we study childhood adversity, we see that some kids are just little tardigrades. For some reason, they don't seem to develop psychopathology as a result of adversity exposure and they may even show positive adaptations to it. - Early studies treated it as an inherent trait or characteristic. - Later studies used a cumulative model, where you count positive and negative measures of the development environment, and if the sum is positive, you say that the subject had resilience. - More recently, it's understood as a dynamic and interactive process involving interactions between the individual and the environment. - And if you remember back to the beginning of the talk, this kind of progression in the conceptual framework may sound awefully familiar. - It's a lot like what we went through with the definition of adversity. - And I think the field is coalescing around the idea that resilience is another facet of the development environment. But we don't yet have a common definition of resilience, let alone a conceptual model that breaks it down into different dimensions that might make different neurodevelopmental predictions. - So if I somehow solve all those other open problems in the next few weeks, it would be good look at the interplay of adversity and resilience in neurodevelopment.
