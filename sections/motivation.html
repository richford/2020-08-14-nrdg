Motivation

* So why would we want to study childhood adversity anyway

Childhood adversity is common in the US

McLaughlin et al. (2012)
* This is data from a nationally representative sample (N > 10,000) * Prevalence rates vary by adversity type * More than half of children will experience at least one of these kinds of adversity before they reach adulthood

Adversity predicts mental health later in life

McLaughlin et al. (2012)
* Number of ACEs vs likelihood of developing mental health problem compared to children who have not had these kinds of experiences. * Dose/response relationship between adverse events and mental disorders later in life

Population Attributable Risk Proportions (PARPs)

Green et al. (2010)
* Population Attributable Risk Proportions (PARPs) are the proportion of all mental disorders in the US that are explained by exposure to adverse experiences. * Another way to think about it is it's the proportion of mental health problems that we could theoretically prevent if we got rid of adversity or could prevent its downstream consequences. * Broken down by age of onset * Almost half of childhood mental health problems attributable to ACEs * PARP descreases as onset age increases but it is still about a quarter for mid-to-late adulthood.
