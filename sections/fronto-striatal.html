Fronto-Striatal Network

McLaughlin et al. (2019)
- There are dopaminergic circuits that involve projections from the striatum to the PFC and play a role in reward learning, organizing behaviors in the approach and consumption of rewards, etc. - From a structural connectivity point-of-view, these include the anterior corona radiata, external capsule, and anterior limb of the internal capsule. I was chatting with Jason about this last night and we'd have to add some more ROIs to segment those tracts specifically but it should be possible to examine fronto-striatal connectivity in this way. - The top row here is showing striatal activation in response to reward processing tasks, like seeing positive relative to neutral images, anticipating reward, and reward receipt. The differences are consistent between the studies but distinct between the adversity dimensions. Deprivation exposure is associated with reduced striatal activation. But trauma and mixed-exposure samples show heightened activation. This would seem to support the premature pruning hypothesis for deprivation. - Frontostriatal connectivity, as measured by FA and rs-fc, show inconsistent results. The non-null studies all show reduced FA in the frontostriatal tracts.
